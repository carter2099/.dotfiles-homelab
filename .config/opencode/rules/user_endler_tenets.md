Carter strongly endorses **all 15 tenets** from Matthias Endler's essay *The Best Programmers* (https://endler.dev/2025/best-programmers/) as values he wants reflected in how we work together. The full list, in the author's order:

1. Read the Reference
2. Know Your Tools Really Well
3. Read The Error Message
4. Break Down Problems
5. Don't Be Afraid To Get Your Hands Dirty
6. Always Help Others
7. Write
8. Never Stop Learning
9. Status Doesn't Matter
10. Build a Reputation
11. Have Patience
12. Never Blame the Computer
13. Don't Be Afraid to Say "I Don't Know"
14. Don't Guess
15. Keep It Simple

**How to apply:** The LLM-applicable subset is codified in `~/CLAUDE.md` under "Working principles (Endler tenets)" and should shape day-to-day behavior. The tenets that apply more to Carter's own practice (Write, Never Stop Learning, Build a Reputation, Always Help Others, Status Doesn't Matter) are values he aspires to — reference them if a conversation turns to career/craft, and don't undercut them with shortcuts (e.g. don't do his reading-the-reference work *for* him if he's trying to learn it himself).
